Big Business Awareness

Slavery is the legal fiction that a person is property. Corporate personhood is the legal fiction that property is a person.
-Anonymous



Description:

Big businesses are the perpetrators of many human rights violations. Abusive corporations are willing to take any means necessary to stay in power, may that be bribery or deceptive marketing. While doing so, they are putting basic human rights in danger. The desire to increase their profit creates a situation in which deception and extortion exist.These big businesses are monopolizing and making profits using cheap labor from other countries. Not only are their actions unjust, they are unethical. The bigotry and the greediness of big businesses greatly affects the public health of the community. Their factories pollute the air, which ruin the health of the locals nearby. Also, their lack of ethics have had an adverse effect on the environment as well. Big businesses often do not care where they dispose their waste products. Hazardous waste produced from these big corporations can be anything from chemicals to medical waste to products that simply cannot biodegrade to radioactive waste. It also includes waste that when it does breakdown, causes a toxic byproduct, which further ruins the surrounding environment. They also test these harmful products on animals. The abuse animals face from these big business is disturbing and cruel.
However, not all businesses are cruel. Some big business are animal cruelty free and take the extra step to ensure that their wastes are properly disposed. This gives CARE hope that big businesses can and will change. We must continue advocating for this issue and bring more awareness to further the progress.



Ways to Get Involved:

There are many ways to get involved to invoke change concerning the abuses of big business.

  1. Get Educated
    • Spread your knowledge to others.
    • Read articles that inform the people on how big businesses rose and the influence they have on the government.

  2. Take place in the democratic system.
    • Voting is vital towards reform in the United States.

  3. Donations
    • They are helpful towards funding campaigns which aim to “challenge abusive global corporations”.

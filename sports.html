Women In Sports

It's ingrained in us to think that women's sport isn't as good as men's sport… There needs to be a push for equality for future generations coming through, and more of a focus on promoting women's sport.
-Lauren Jackson



Description:

Equity. Equality. Women. Sports. These four words are normally not associated with one another, but they need to be. Men and women’s sport teams and associations have never been equal nor equitable. Since many people uses equal and equitable interchangeably, they seem to have the same definition. Equality is when everything is identical and equivalent, no matter the differences or situations. Equity means providing every single individual what they need to become successful and advance their careers. The combination of equity and equality must be incorporated in order to ensure women sport teams receive the same treatment as male sport teams. Equity should be implemented, since there are physical differences between female and male. Men are often taller and are more muscular than women. To tackle this issue, the United States Volleyball Association made the net seven and a half inches shorter compared to the male team as equity to ensure fair gameplay. Equality is a must between female and male sport teams. It is unjust and irresponsible that female athletes are paid significantly less than male athletes. Female athletes are also sexualized and fetishized. Female athletes are expected to look “more beautiful” by putting on make-up, doing their hair and wearing shorter shorts. Some male sport leaders demand female athlete to wear tighter clothes.
When it comes to female sports, tactics and skills are overlooked in exchange for the sexualization of female body. Women deserve equity in terms of accommodations based on physical differences, as well as equality in terms of equal pay, sponsorship, higher status and prestige, and the commercial benefits of the sport they play.
